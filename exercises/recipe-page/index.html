Apple Pie Danishes

Nothing beats the smell of homemade danishes fresh out of the oven, especially when they look and taste like they’re straight from a bakery! These apple cream cheese danishes are flaky, buttery, and filled with warm spiced apples and creamy, sweetened cream cheese. Whether for breakfast, dessert, or a cozy afternoon snack, these danishes are a treat you’ll want to make again and again.

Apple Filling

The apple filling is the heart of this recipe. Start by peeling and dicing Granny Smith apples into small cubes – they hold their shape beautifully during baking and have a slight tartness that balances the sweetness. Cook the apples in butter until they soften, then add cinnamon, brown sugar, and a pinch of salt to enhance their flavor.

Now, here’s where the magic happens: to thicken the apple mixture, you’ll need to create a cornstarch slurry. Mix the cornstarch with a little bit of water before adding it to the apples. This step is key – by dissolving the cornstarch in water first, you ensure that it blends smoothly into the apples without clumping, giving the mixture that perfect syrupy consistency. Finish off with a splash of lemon juice for brightness and vanilla extract to tie all the flavors together.

PRO TIP: Don’t overcook the apples. You want them tender but not mushy, so they hold up in the danish.

The Cream Cheese Filling

A danish wouldn’t be complete without that luscious cream cheese filling. Make sure your cream cheese is softened to room temperature – this is important because it ensures a smooth, lump-free texture. Cold cream cheese will stay clumpy, and you won’t get that rich, velvety filling we’re after.

Whip the softened cream cheese with sugar, an egg yolk, and a splash of vanilla extract (for extra depth of flavor!). You can use a stand mixer or a hand-held mixer for this step. Either way, mix until the filling is creamy and light, with no lumps in sight. This adds the perfect tangy sweetness to complement the spiced apples.

PRO TIP: If you’re in a hurry and forget to soften your cream cheese, you can microwave it for just 10-15 seconds to take the chill off.

Shaping and Rolling the Puff Pastry

Now let’s talk puff pastry! For this recipe, you’ll need puff pastry sheets, and here’s a tip: roll them out just a little bit thinner. This ensures a lighter, crispier pastry and gives the danishes that bakery-style flakiness. Roll out each sheet of puff pastry just a little. This step also helps you get more danishes from each sheet.

Once rolled out, you can cut the puff pastry into circles, rectangles, or squares, depending on your preference. For a traditional look, cut circles using a round cutter. To keep the centers from puffing up too much during baking, use a smaller cutter to score a circle about 3/4 of the way through the center of each piece. If you’re opting for rectangles or squares, you can use a knife to score a slightly smaller rectangle or square – either way, they’ll turn out beautiful!

PRO TIP Work quickly with the puff pastry and keep it cold. If it warms up too much, pop it in the fridge for a few minutes before continuing. The cold pastry will rise better and get those gorgeous flaky layers.

Assembling the Danishes

Time to assemble! Spread a spoonful amount of the cream cheese filling onto the center of each puff pastry piece, staying within the inner circle you scored. Then, add a good amount of the apple filling on top. Don’t skimp on the filling – you want each bite to have that perfect balance of creamy, spiced apples and buttery pastry!

Brush the edges of the pastry with egg wash to give the danishes a glossy, golden finish. For an extra touch of sweetness and a little sparkle, sprinkle some granulated sugar on the edges before baking.

Baking and Finishing Touches

Bake your danishes in a preheated oven until the puff pastry is golden brown and crispy, and the filling is bubbling. You’ll know they’re ready when the edges are puffed up and flaky, and the apples look beautifully caramelized. Once they’re out of the oven, let them cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. This helps keep the bottoms from getting soggy.

Icing Drizzle

While the danishes cool, whip up a quick icing. Mix powdered sugar with milk or heavy cream, and a splash of vanilla extract until smooth. The icing should be thick but pourable. Drizzle the icing over the danishes while they’re still warm but not hot, so it sets nicely and doesn’t slide off. This step is optional, but it gives the danishes that signature sweet finish you’d expect from a bakery treat.

Variations and Tips

  • Different shapes: Circles are classic, but feel free to switch things up by cutting the puff pastry into squares or rectangles. Rectangles are great for folding over the filling for an easy-to-handle danish, while squares can be folded into beautiful parcels.
  • Fruit options Swap the apples for pears, peaches, or even mixed berries if you want to switch up the flavor. Adjust the cooking time for the fruit filling depending on the texture – softer fruits won’t need as long.
  • Make-ahead tips:You can make the apple filling ahead of time and store it in the fridge for up to a day. That way, when you’re ready to bake, all you have to do is assemble and pop them in the oven.

Serving Suggestions

These danishes are best served slightly warm, with a fresh drizzle of icing or a dusting of powdered sugar. Pair them with a hot cup of coffee or tea for the perfect cozy morning or afternoon treat. They also freeze well – just reheat them in the oven for a few minutes to get that fresh-baked taste again!

These apple cream cheese danishes bring all the bakery vibes right to your kitchen. With flaky puff pastry, creamy vanilla-spiked cream cheese, and perfectly spiced apples, they’re a treat that’s as impressive as it is easy to make!

Apple Pie Danishes

These apple cream cheese danishes taste like they came straight from a bakery, but they’re so easy to make at home! Flaky, buttery puff pastry with a creamy layer of sweetened cream cheese and tender cinnamon-spiced apples – every bite is a dream. Perfect for breakfast, dessert, or a cozy afternoon treat!

Prep Time: 40 Minutes Cook Time: 20 Minutes
Total Time: 1 hour Yields: 8 to 10 Minutes

Ingredients

Apple Pie Filling:

  • 2 large Granny Smith apples, peeled and cubed
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/3 cup light brown sugar
  • 1 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • pinch of salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon water

Cheese Filling:

  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup granulated white sugar
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Egg Wash:

  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tablespoon whole milk

Icing Glaze

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons whole milk or heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Other

  • 2 puff pastry sheets
  • granulated white sugar, for sprinkling

Instructions

Prepare Apple FIlling

  1. In a medium pan, melt the butter over medium heat.
  2. Add the cubed apples, brown sugar, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt. Cook until the apples soften, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Stir in the vanilla extract, lemon juice, cornstarch, and water. Continue cooking until the mixture thickens, about 2 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and let the apple mixture cool.

Prepare Cheese Filling

  1. In a bowl, mix the softened cream cheese, sugar, egg yolk, and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. Set aside.

Assemble Danishes:

  1. Roll out the puff pastry sheets on a floured surface and cut each sheet into circles using a large cookie cutter or a glass.
  2. Using a smaller cutter, lightly score a smaller circle about 3/4 of the way through the dough in the center of each larger circle. This will help keep the center from rising too much during baking.
  3. Spoon about 1 tablespoon of the cream cheese filling into the center of each scored circle.
  4. Top the cream cheese mixture with a spoonful of the cooled apple filling.
  5. Whisk the egg and milk together in a small bowl. Brush the outer edges of the pastry with the egg wash.
  6. Sprinkle a little granulated sugar over the edges.

Bake the Danishes:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Bake the danishes for 15-20 minutes or until the puff pastry is golden and flaky.

The Icing:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and milk or cream until smooth. Adjust the consistency with more milk if necessary.
  2. Once the danishes are out of the oven and fully cooled, drizzle the icing over them and enjoy!
